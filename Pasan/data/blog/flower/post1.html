Learn The Secret Language Of Flowers

 

Flowers are the best mood lifters and are truly perfect for any occasion. Whether you want to declare your love to someone or condole with them; whether you’re happy or sad; whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or just because—flowers can instantly change your mood. However, there is so much more than what meets the eye with beautiful flowers. Every flower has a different meaning behind it, almost as though these gorgeous blooms have a language of their own. 
Read this blog to discover the secret language of flowers and see if you have been gifting the right flower for the right occasion! 

 

Alstroemeria 
Also known as the Peruvian Lily due to its close resemblance to a lily, the alstroemeria was named after a Swedish astronomer who discovered the flower when trekking through Spain. This long-lasting flower comes in a variety of colours and its longevity represents wealth and prosperity. This blossom is also said to symbolise friendship.


Anthurium 
This dark pink, almost red, flower comes with many exotic names like Flamingo Flower, is one of the most stunning varieties of tropical flowers. The flower has a shiny and waxy feel to it with dark green leaves. Anthuriums are thought to bring luck and protect against evil. Legend has it that when the flower closes its petals it means that rain is approaching!


Bird Of Paradise 
The bird of paradise is as exotic as it sounds and it is a cacophony of colour in a single flower. This bloom got its name for resembling a colourful bird and it is best known for its vivid orange and blue streaks. While you can occasionally find white bird of paradise flowers they are extremely rare and somewhat take away the beauty of this hypnotic blossom. The flower is a symbol of freedom and joy and is said to be the ultimate emblem of paradise. 


Calla Lily 
Calla lilies were named after the Greek name for beauty- calla- and this flower was so beautiful that Venus, the goddess of love and beauty was envious of it! White is the most common colour of this flower as it symbolises purity and innocence and is a favourite option for wedding flower bouquets. 

 

Carnation 
Carnations are one of Lassana Flora’s most popular flowers and has a history which dates back nearly 2,000 years ago. This tiered flower is believed to get its name from the word coronation because the ancient Greeks had used carnations in ceremonial crowns. However, it is also said that carnation is derived from the Latin word carnis which means flesh, in regard to its original light pink hue. 
Carnations come in several colours and each colour suits a different occasion and feeling. 
-    Red Carnations: love, pride and admiration 

-    Pink Carnations: love of a woman but most commonly a mother 

-    White Carnations: innocence and pure love 

-    Yellow Carnations: disdain, rejection or disappointment
 

Gerbera 
Belonging to the daisy family gerbera flowers convey cheerfulness and are flowers for celebrations. Gerberas are meant to symbolise the simple beauty of a happy life and are heavily associated with children's innocence. 


Lily 
Lilies are one of the most fragrant flowers and these flowers are most commonly associated with devotion and purity. Lilies are entrenched in mythology and are strongly linked to the goddess Hera, wife of Zeus and Goddess of Marriage and the Family. Lilies are symbolic of motherhood and eternal love, but like many blossoms, they come in different colours each having its own meaning. 
-    White Lilies: modesty and virginity, 

-    Orange (Tiger) Lilies: confidence, wealth and pride

-    Yellow Lilies: gratefulness 

-    Pink Lilies: prosperity and abundance

-    Red Lilies: passion

Orchid 
Orchids are heavily coveted flowers and this delicate bloom represents love, luxury, and delicate beauty, however, different cultures perceive the orchid in different ways. To the ancient Greeks, orchids were associated with virility and it was believed that if fathers of unborn children ate large orchid tubers the baby would be a boy, while small tubers meant they would have a girl. While the Aztecs combined vanilla orchid with chocolate to make an elixir which was believed to increase power and strength. However, during the Victorian era, orchids were regarded as a symbol of luxury. 

Rose 
Finally, roses are well known for being the most romantic flowers. Roses symbolise love, and the different colours of roses depict love in its various forms. 
-    Red Roses: a red rose is the ultimate expression of love, which is why bouquets of red roses are the favourite Valentine’s Day gift! Red roses are for deep emotions whether it be love, longing or desire, but nowadays it is mainly used as a declaration of love.

-    White Roses: the colour white is heavily linked with purity, chastity and innocence. White roses signify new beginnings and are great for weddings. However, white flowers are also a sign of spirituality and are used to convey humility or sympathy.

-    Yellow Roses: yellow roses are for joy, warmth and happiness. A symbol of friendship and caring, the yellow rose is for platonic love and friendship.

-    Pink Roses: pink is a delicate colour and pinks roses are used to convey gentle emotions like joy and gratitude. Light pink roses have a certain innocence to them while deep pink roses depict elegance and grace. 

There is a flower for every mood and emotion, and by understanding the symbolic meaning of each flower you will learn a language that not many people know. Send someone a sentimental message through the language of flowers today at Lassana Flora.
